TRIANGLE PALM

Basic Information

Common Name: Triangle Palm
Scientific Name: Dypsis decaryi
Family: Arecaceae
Origin: Madagascar
Plant Type: Ornamental palm tree

Identification Features

The leaves emerge from three distinct sides of the trunk.
This arrangement gives the palm its unique triangular appearance.

Growing Conditions

-Requires full sunlight.
-Grows best in well-drained soil.
-Tolerates drought once established.

Benefits
  • Enhances landscape beauty.
  • Adds a tropical look to gardens.
  • Requires relatively low maintenance.

Common Problems
  • Root rot due to poor drainage.
  • Nutrient deficiencies.
  • Cold weather damage.

Propagation

Triangle Palm is mainly propagated through seeds.

Care Tips
  • Plant in a sunny location.
  • Water regularly when young.
  • Ensure proper drainage.

Interesting Facts
  • Native to Madagascar.
  • One of the most distinctive palm species.
  • The leaves naturally form a triangular pattern.

Blooming Period

 The Triangle Palm (Dypsis decaryi) actively grows during the warm, high-light months of spring and summer, peaking from March to September. During this time, the palm pushes out new fronds and benefits from regular monthly or quarterly palm fertilization.
  • Active Growth Period: March through September (when temperatures are warm and sunlight is highest).
  • Fertilization Schedule: Apply a slow-release granular or liquid palm fertilizer containing micronutrients during spring, summer, and fall to support frond development.
  • Watering: Needs more water during the hot summer growing months, but requires excellent drainage and is drought-tolerant once established.
