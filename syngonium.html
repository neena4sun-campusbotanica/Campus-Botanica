SYNGONIUM

Basic Information

Common Name: Arrowhead Plant
Scientific Name: Syngonium podophyllum
Family: Araceae
Origin: Central and South America
Plant Type: Evergreen climbing houseplant

Identification Features

Young leaves are arrow-shaped.
The leaves become more lobed as the plant matures.

Growing Conditions

-Prefers bright indirect light.
-Requires moist, well-drained soil.
-Grows best in warm and humid conditions.

Benefits
  • Popular indoor ornamental plant.
  • Easy to maintain.
  • Adds beauty and greenery to homes.

Common Problems
  • Root rot due to overwatering.
  • Brown leaf tips caused by low humidity.
  • Spider mites and mealybugs.

Propagation

Can be easily propagated through stem cuttings in water or soil.

Care Tips
  • Keep soil slightly moist.
  • Avoid direct sunlight.
  • Provide support for climbing growth.

Interesting Facts
  • Its leaf shape changes as it matures.
  • Can grow as a climber or trailing plant.
  • One of the most popular indoor plants worldwide.

Blooming Period

 Syngonium plants experience their peak growing season from March to September (Spring and Summer), during which they put out rapid, trailing foliage growth.
  • Spring (March – May): The plant’s metabolic rate spikes as temperatures and daylight increase. Growth/Spread: Moderate to fast production of new foliage. Action: Start feeding monthly with a balanced liquid fertilizer and repot if rootbound.
  • Summer (June – August): The peak of the growing and spreading phase. Growth/Spread: Rapid vining and leaf expansion. Action: Water frequently as the soil dries and pinch the tips to encourage a bushy spread.
  • Fall (September – November): Transitioning out of the active growing season. Growth/Spread: Slows down significantly. Action: Stop fertilizing completely by mid-fall as the plant prepares for dormancy.
  • Winter (December – February): Dormancy period. Growth/Spread: Little to no growth; may drop older leaves. Action: Cut back on watering; do not fertilize.
